Craftsman Joinery for Ponteland Properties

Ponteland's housing stock is wonderfully varied — from 19th-century stone cottages in the village centre to modern detached family homes in newer developments. Both deserve joinery that's made to measure, not taken off a shelf.

For period properties, we can match original profiles, reproduce traditional mouldings, and use materials that are sympathetic to the building's character. For contemporary homes, we design clean, precise woodwork that complements a modern interior. Either way, every piece is made to your exact specification in our own workshop and fitted by the same team that built it.

Because we make everything ourselves, we're not constrained by standard sizes or catalogue options — your joinery fits your home precisely, not the other way around.

Common Questions

From Ponteland homeowners about bespoke joinery

Can you match the existing joinery in my period Ponteland home?

Yes — this is one of our particular strengths. Victorian and Edwardian properties have specific skirting profiles, architrave styles, and panelling details that we reproduce accurately. We'll examine your existing joinery before quoting to ensure the new work integrates seamlessly.

What timber species do you work with?

We work in a wide range of species: oak and ash for natural finishes, pine and MDF for painted work, and hardwood timbers for feature pieces. We'll recommend the right material for your application at the design stage.

How long does bespoke joinery take to make and install?

Lead times depend on the complexity of the piece — a fitted wardrobe suite typically takes 4–6 weeks from order to installation; a full staircase can take 6–8 weeks. We'll confirm your programme at the quote stage and keep you updated throughout the workshop build.

Can joinery work be combined with an extension or loft conversion project?

Absolutely — and this is often the most cost-effective approach. Having your staircase, doors, and fitted furniture made as part of a larger build project means one team, one contract, and one point of contact. We coordinate all elements to ensure the joinery arrives on site at exactly the right stage.
